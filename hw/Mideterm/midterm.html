What is gelato?

For many gelato is just another way of saying ice cream but these two sweet and savory desserts aren't exactly the same. If you ever test trial the two desserts side by side you will clearly notice that gelato is much creamier than ice cream. This is because while both desserts contain milk, cream, and sugar, the ratio of these ingredients is not the same. Authentic gelato has a heavy emphasis on milk rather than cream. This along with sparse use of egg yolks in gelato are what makes its dense conistency and silky texture.

Fun Facts

The exact origin of gelato is a little hazy, no one exactly knows who created it first but many place the dessert's birth place in Florence. The word itself is derived from the Italian word 'congelato' which means frozen. The first ever gelato machine was created in Sicily in 1686 by a man of the name Francesco Propocio dei Coltelli. Yet, the populaity of gelato wasn't really present until the 1920's. However, the industy has boomed in recent decades especially in the US, where it has annually generated 2 billion in revenue in recent years.

Gelato is not simply confined to be eaten in a cone or cup. As seen in the picture, ordering gelato in a brioche bun is a very popular way to have gelato, as it essenially creates the effect of a freshly made ice cream sandwich. Furthermore, people often ask for freshly made espresso to be poured over their gelato, creating an entirely different delicacy known as 'Affogato'. Lastly, there are Italian versions of milkshakes with gelato being blended with milk to create a 'Frulato'.
